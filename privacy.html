Plain summary: 21 Pro does not collect your name, email, location, or any personal information. We use anonymous IDs for analytics and subscription management, and we show ads to free users with your permission. Full details below.

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how 21 Pro: Blackjack Trainer ("the App," "we," "us") handles data when you use the App on iPhone or iPad.

We built 21 Pro as a training tool, not a data platform. We collect the minimum data needed to run the app, fix bugs, serve ads to free users, and manage subscriptions. Nothing more.

2. Data We Collect

The table below covers all data types collected or generated when you use the App. None are sold.

Data Type Purpose Tracking?
Anonymous User ID Analytics (PostHog), subscription management (RevenueCat) No
Device Advertising ID (IDFA) Targeted advertising (AdMob) — with ATT consent only Yes*
Purchase History Subscription management (RevenueCat, Apple StoreKit) No
Product Interaction / Usage Events App improvement and analytics (PostHog) No
Session Recordings UX analysis (PostHog) No
Ad Interaction Data Ad performance measurement (AdMob) Yes*

* IDFA and ad interaction data are used for tracking only with your explicit App Tracking Transparency (ATT) consent.

Game progress, session history, settings, and personal bests are stored locally on your device only and are never sent to our servers.

3. Data We Do Not Collect

We have no access to:

  • Name, email address, phone number, or physical address
  • Location data (precise or approximate)
  • Health, financial, or sensitive personal data
  • Contacts, photos, camera, or microphone
  • Browsing history or search history
  • User-generated content
  • Biometric data

4. Third-Party Services

The App integrates the following SDKs. Each operates under its own privacy policy.

RevenueCat Privacy Policy ↗
Purpose: In-app subscription and purchase management.
Data collected: Anonymous user ID, purchase history, subscription status. No personal information. RevenueCat processes this data solely to verify and manage your Edge subscription.
PostHog Privacy Policy ↗
Purpose: Product analytics and session replay to improve the App.
Data collected: Anonymous user ID, in-app events (taps, screen views, feature usage), session recordings. Processed on PostHog US servers (us.i.posthog.com). No personal information is attached to these events. Session recordings capture UI interactions only — no keyboard input or personal data is recorded.
Google AdMob Privacy Policy ↗
Purpose: Rewarded video ads shown to free-tier users at natural break points (between runs, optional run saves).
Data collected: Device Advertising Identifier (IDFA), ad interaction data (views, completions, clicks). We show Apple's ATT prompt before any advertising tracking occurs. If you decline, AdMob shows non-personalized ads and does not use your IDFA. Upgrading to Edge removes all ads.
Firebase Crashlytics (Google) Privacy Policy ↗
Purpose: Crash reporting and stability diagnostics.
Data collected: Crash logs, stack traces, device model, iOS version, app version. No personal information is included in crash reports. Data is used only to identify and fix bugs.
Apple StoreKit Apple Privacy Policy ↗
Purpose: In-app purchase transaction processing via Apple's infrastructure.
Data collected: Apple manages all billing data. We receive only transaction verification tokens — no payment card details, Apple ID, or personal information passes through our systems.

5. Advertising & Tracking (ATT)

Free-tier users see rewarded video ads served by Google AdMob. Ads appear at natural break points and are never shown mid-hand.

App Tracking Transparency (ATT)

Before any advertising tracking begins, we show Apple's standard ATT permission prompt, as required by iOS 14.5 and later.

  • Allow Tracking: AdMob may use your IDFA to serve personalized ads and measure performance across apps and websites.
  • Ask App Not to Track: AdMob shows non-personalized ads. Your IDFA is not used or shared with ad networks for tracking.

What is and isn't used for tracking

With ATT consent, the following are used for tracking: Device Advertising ID (IDFA), ad interaction data.

The following are never used for tracking: purchase history, product interaction and usage data, anonymous user IDs.

Opting out

Change your preference at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → 21 Pro. Upgrading to Edge removes all ads entirely.

6. Purchases & Subscriptions

All payment processing is handled by Apple. We do not store or access your payment card details, Apple ID, or billing address. RevenueCat receives an anonymous confirmation token from Apple to manage your entitlement — no payment details are included.

Edge subscription options:

  • Monthly — $6.99/month
  • Annual — $49.99/year
  • Lifetime — $149.99 (one-time, non-recurring)

Monthly and annual plans auto-renew unless cancelled at least 24 hours before the end of the current period. Manage or cancel at Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions.

7. GDPR — European Users

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, GDPR applies.

Legal bases for processing

  • Legitimate interests — crash reporting and anonymous analytics to maintain and improve the App.
  • Contract performance — subscription management to deliver the service you purchased.
  • Consent — advertising tracking via AdMob/IDFA, which requires your explicit ATT consent.

Your rights

You have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or port your data, and to withdraw consent at any time. To exercise these rights, email EpochBrandFeedback@gmail.com. We respond within 30 days. You may also lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

Because most data in 21 Pro is anonymous and stored locally, many GDPR requests can be fulfilled simply by deleting the App. We will confirm this where applicable.

8. CCPA — California Residents

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), California residents have specific rights.

Categories of personal information we collect

  • Identifiers — anonymous user ID; device advertising ID (with ATT consent)
  • Commercial information — purchase history, subscription status
  • Internet or network activity — app usage events, session recordings
  • Inferences — none. We do not build user profiles.

Do we sell personal information?

No. We do not sell personal information. We share limited data with service providers listed in Section 4 for operational purposes only.

Do we share for cross-context behavioral advertising?

We share Device Advertising ID (IDFA) with Google AdMob for advertising, but only with your ATT consent. Opt out at any time via Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.

Your California rights

  • Right to know what personal information we collect and how we use it
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information (we collect none)
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights

Submit a CCPA request to EpochBrandFeedback@gmail.com. We respond within 45 days.

9. Children's Privacy

21 Pro is rated 17+ on the App Store and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided information through the App, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. Data Retention & Deletion

Game data (progress, stats, settings) is stored locally on your device. Deleting the App removes it entirely.

For data held by third-party services:

  • PostHog — anonymous event data retained for up to 12 months
  • RevenueCat — purchase records retained as required for subscription management and tax compliance
  • Firebase Crashlytics — crash reports retained for 90 days
  • AdMob — ad interaction data retained per Google's data retention policies

To request deletion of data associated with your anonymous ID, email EpochBrandFeedback@gmail.com.

11. Encryption

21 Pro uses SHA-256 via Apple's CryptoKit for internal deck verification. This is exempt encryption under US Export Administration Regulations (EAR). We have made the applicable declaration for availability in France per App Store requirements. All data transmitted to third-party services is encrypted in transit using TLS.

If changes are material, we will note this in the App. Continued use after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
